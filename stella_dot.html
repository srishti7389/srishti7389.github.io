Stella & Dot - Category Pages
Case Study

The objective of this project was to redesign the category page and make it more engaging/ social by integrating user generated content with inventory & model images, elevate the design of the page and use brand guidelines provided.

Initial findings

After going through the heatmaps for the page the aspects that stood out were:
  1. Clicks-heatmap clearly shows a significant drop in clicks after the 3rd row of products.
  2. But,there is only a gradual decrease in the number of people scrolling past the 3rd row, as derived from the graph below.
  3. The current design is clean and consistent. However generates significantly lower CTR on products below the first fold. As a user scrolls through, she might become overwhelmed with the options and it would become harder for her to choose (paradox of choice?). We need to help her make a decision by either making certain products stand out more than others, or adding social validation to reinforce her choice.

Market Research

Possible Directions for Redesign after doing the Market Research

  • Incorporating UGC with some context to the product will reinforce a user’s preference in the product (like Modcloth). Having UGC without context might leave the user confused/distracted from the purpose of the page.
  • Showing social validation to make products stand out will again help users in making a choice. Ways to do this could be:-
    1. products “viewed” or “bought” by others - (like Expedia)
    2. most liked on FB - (like Levi’s)
    3. user reviews - (like Airbnb)
  • Making the site look dynamic will increase engagement (like ESPNCricinfo, Tieks)

Based on these findings I went on to quickly sketch multiple directions for the interface design and from over 10 skecthes narrowed down to 2 options which were validating the above points the most.

Option 1

The first direction was to have a Slider at the top of the page displaying a user image along with her comment. This would provide a social proof to the user as soon as she lands on the website. A “Trending Section” after a few rows of products with curated UGC interspersed with model images, shows the product on hover.

Pros

  • Clean, consistent layout of inventory.
  • Having a trending section after a few rows of products breaks browser monotony.
  • Top slider should increase user interaction with popular styles, giving them immediate social proof.

Cons

  • Because no products have been displayed before the top slider, it might feel out of context.
  • Hiding inventory behind a ‘view all’ button might reduce checkout conversions as users prefer scrolling to clicking and would not be too keen on clicking a button to see more inventory.
  • Above-the-fold real estate is expensive, and is being taken away by UGC.

Option 2

UGC shown in-line with the products, with context, so when a product gas been displayed a UGC with that product will appear.
A “ticker” that shows thumbnails of curated user submitted images, along with the user’s id, and the name of the product she is sporting.
For social validation, prominently show number of times product has been bought or shared on social media. This option maximizes chance to capture viewer’s clicks from a user image.

Pros

  • By adding a “shared x times” data on a hover, we are providing users with immediate social proof.
  • UGC in this option islinked with product images contextually and it reinforces a user’s preference immediately.
  • Ticker with images shared on social media breaks browsing monotony, provides a “social” avenue to capture user clicks.

Cons

  • Product layout may not be as clean and symmetric as Option 1.
  • User images need to be curated to match the look and feel of the category page.

After careful consideration of the pros and cons of both the options, I decided to use Option 2 as the direction.

Design Process

Version 1

On presenting the Version 1 to Product managers and design team, along with the research to back up my design decisions, I was given some constructive feedback and the areas that needed work were:

  1. UGC spanning over 2 columns of product images can be very distracting and takes away from the inventory.
  2. Circular UGC thumbnails don’t relate to instagram and can be confusing.
  3. The “Instagrammed over 5 times” makes the product description cluttered and needs to be placed elsewehere.
