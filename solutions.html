Solutions Showcase

The Solutions Showcase is a platform for companies to share sustainable ideas with other companies to reduce our carbon footprint and the use of non-renewable resources. The task was to design a web interface allowing users to browse through thousands of submitted solutions and adapt the ones that fit their requirement. The product also incorporated a platform for companies to upload their own solutions. Since there was a lot of information to be presented, I created a well-balanced architecture providing users with what they need, when they need it. The final experince was simple, fast, adaptive and intuitive.

My Role

We had 4 weeks to push the project from start to finish. I worked closely with a Project Manager and a Business Developer to uncover business needs and translate ideas and concepts into features that address user motivations. More specifically my contributions to the project were—
  • Prioritizing and negotiating features based on the strategic objectives, business requirements and feasibility.
  • Working with the project manager to define a scope that foresees potential conflicts and rough spots.
  • Documenting functional specifications after exploring user scenarios.
  • Working with the project manager to create a strong structure, organizing information in a manner that provides intuitive access to content.
  • Designing and presenting wireframes and prototypes to the Executive Director and Board members.

Process

Market Research

The market for presenting solutions to environmental issues was being led by companies like OpenIDEO and VirtualExpo.The first step was performing a thorough analysis between these market leaders to uncover areas where we could stand out, and learn from strategies these products use that make them successful. We compared these to our initial list of features that was set by the business. Our competitors were lacking a few features including customizing solution profile by users and certain searching limitations like “search by a category” and “search by a company”.

Defining Clear Strategic Objectives

To bring more clarity to our objective and focus on features that were truly necessary in the time frame allotted. I used a red route analysis graph that highlighted which features are worth spending UXD efforts on.

Compromises had to be made. Even though stakeholders strongly suggested the need to reduce redundancy in similar solutions, we could not afford to spend time on it. We had limited resources, had we proceeded with the feature we would have need to manual filter out redundant solutions.
