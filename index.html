    Branding  ·  UI

    Dance Off is a boutique dance studio based in Palo Alto, California. It is a fairly new dance studio that promises to teach dancers and non-dancers the power and therapy of dance movement. The company wanted to create a strong brand that resonates with their values and makes them distinguishable.The key words from the initial meetings were— strong, approachable, passionate and depicting movement.

    Solutions Showcase

    UI  ·  UX

    The Solutions Showcase is a platform for companies to share sustainable ideas with other companies to reduce our carbon footprint and increase awareness about reducing the use of non-renewable resources. The task was to design an experience that is easy to use and adapt. Since we had a lot of information to present, we needed to create a well-balanced architecture providing users with just what they need, when they need it. The interface needed to be simple, fast, adaptive and intuitive.

    Earth Day

    Branding  ·  Illustration

    Earth day is an annual event celebrated on April 22. On this day, events worldwide are held to demonstrate support for environmental protection. It was first celebrated in 1970, and is now coordinated globally by the Earth Day Network and celebrated in more than 192 countries each year. The SF Earth Day team wanted to promote the Street Fair by showcasing Earth Day posters, banners and flyers across the city as well as social media. The Street Fair was to host activities like dancing, concerts, food stalls, etc. The design was briefed as “simplistic, something that appealed to the likes of young kids and older adults”.

    Topdown Tours

    Branding  ·  Graphic Design

    Tony, a Bay Area native founded Topdown Tours, a luxury tour service along with his wife.The tours were offered in Mini Coopers and were custom made to fit the tourists needs/interests. He knows the Bay Area like the back of his hand and it is his passion to share the rich, diverse culture with everyone who decides to visit! Tony wanted to have a logo designed for the company along with a brochure, which he would leave at hotel desks and restaurants and also a design for his website. He specifically wanted his Mini Cooper to be promoted as he wanted people to know how luxurious the tours were going to be.
